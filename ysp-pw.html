In my art practice I hone in on the overlooked. Close observation can reveal surroundings that are rich in strange and fascinating details. From the thin, clear plastic bag dancing in the air on a summer afternoon, to a single shoe in the middle of a busy road (the why of which I'll never know). Photography is a one way to extract this richness, writing or language is another. My practice also deals with making the familiar less so. I think subconscously, I'm always looking for a compelling image.

The photographs above were made in summer 2019. I used a teleidoscope (a kaleidoscope with a clear sphere at one end) as a lens. The teleidoscope turns the immediate environment into a cornucopia of patterns, which fascinates me. These particular photographs haven't been shown anywhere else and are part of a larger series.
